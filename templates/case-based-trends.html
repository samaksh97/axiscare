The State of The States

Around 9 States in the US have hospitals that faced >20% critical shortage of staffing daily on average for the last 2 years consistently

We can clearly see how gaps in the system are revealed when COVID cases and deaths start to rise, hospitals scramble for staff.

All States Are Not Equal

  • All states are not equipped in the same way to manage staffing e.g. Hospitals in Pennsylvania average about 70-80% utilization and face much lower staff shortages as compared to Wyoming which face higher shortages even with just 45% inpatient bed utilization.
  • We can see a similar pattern in Kansas vs North Carolina

The Good, the Bad and the Ugly

  • Smaller and remote states such as Virgin Islands, Rhode islands and New Mexico top the charts for facing critical staff shortages.
  • Florida, New York and Illinois outperform other states in finding staff even in the face of high COVID-19 caseload, there is a need to explore how they are keeping up their infrastructure in place
  • Georgia, Arizona and Virgina are states that need the government attention and help to allocate the staff needed to manage patients.
